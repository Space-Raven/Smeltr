## History

### Origins (1850)

Pjorg was developed in 1850 by Dr. Francis "Kegsmasher" MounteBlanc Fort-Pitchard-on-glenn Foxworthy Fitz Willams (no relation), then serving as headmaster of the Oslo School for Disreputable Orphans. The game was originally conceived as a structured diversion for the children under his tutelage, many of whom were suffering from moose bites, and for whom conventional recreation had proven impractical. It proved immediately compelling beyond the school's walls. Within months it had spread throughout the city of Oslo and into the surrounding countryside, attaining a popularity that Dr. Francis himself is said to have found "gratifying, though somewhat alarming."

The original ruleset was recorded by Dr. Francis in a hand-bound volume known as the **CODEX MALIFICARUM**, which served as the sole authoritative reference for the game during its early years. Dr. Francis was notably insistent, on multiple recorded occasions, that the binding material was not skin or anything of that nature — describing it only as "something totally different."

---

### The Oslo Fire Schism of 1854

On the evening of **November 18th, 1854**, a number of outraged clergymen forcibly entered the private residence of Dr. Francis MounteBlanc Fort-Pitchard-on-glenn Foxworthy Fitz Willams (no relation) and seized the CODEX MALIFICARUM. The precise events of that night remain unknown. By morning, a violent blaze had consumed the residence entirely.

Neither Dr. Francis nor any of the intruders were ever seen again.

This event — known formally as **The Oslo Fire Schism of 1854** — is considered the most significant moment in Pjorg history. In its aftermath, Pjorg was outlawed across the region under **penalty of death**.

*Historian's note: It remains unclear whether the events of that evening occurred in the context of an active game of Pjorg or as the result of unrelated clerical outrage. The distinction has bearing on several points of liability that have never been formally adjudicated, including the matter of a local stroopwaffle stand destroyed during the night's events.*

The destruction of the CODEX MALIFICARUM also produced an enduring consequence: in the absence of an authoritative text, two independent factions emerged, each claiming to preserve the true rules of Pjorg as transmitted through oral tradition and surviving correspondence. These factions gave rise to the **two distinct rulesets** known and practiced to this day. The ideological rift between their governing bodies — the **Grand Pjorg Consortium** and the **Sovereign Fellowship of Pjorg Purists** — has never been formally resolved.

---

### The Ban Years (1854–1892)

For nearly four decades, Pjorg was practiced in secret, its players subject to the harshest legal penalties. Underground Pjorg networks maintained continuity of play throughout this period, and it is during these years that many of the game's regional variations emerged, as isolated communities developed rules independently of one another.

In **1892**, following decades of public pressure, the prohibition was formally reviewed. A parliamentary commission concluded that maintaining a death penalty for participation in what had become Sweden's most popular pastime was, in their words, **"draconian"** and **"not sustainable for the population."** The ban was lifted that same year. Pjorg emerged from the shadows immediately and with considerable enthusiasm.

---

### The First World Championship and the Sarajevo Incident (1914)

The **First World Pjorg Championship** was convened on **June 28th, 1914**, in Sarajevo, Bosnia, drawing competitors from across Europe in what was anticipated to be a historic celebration of the game's international reach.

The match was never completed. During a pivotal riddle sequence, a critical misinterpretation by one or more parties set off a chain of events whose full consequences are well-documented in the historical record. The game was abandoned mid-play. Whether the match is considered ongoing, forfeited, or void is a question that remains formally unresolved by both the Grand Pjorg Consortium and the Sovereign Fellowship of Pjorg Purists, who disagree on the applicable rule, naturally.

More critically, the CODEX MALIFICARUM — present at the championship in the custody of the Grand Pjorg Consortium — was lost in the confusion. Its disappearance created another vacuum of authority, and in the years that followed, a heretical third ruleset emerged from **southern Albania**. Its origins, authorship, and contents are not known to the general public. Its practice is **forbidden**. The Albanian sect was ultimately eliminated following a rare cessation of hostilities between the Grand Pjorg Consortium and the Sovereign Fellowship of Pjorg Purists, who agreed to a brief ceasefire in order to undertake a joint military annexation of the country. The operation was considered a success. However, disputes over resource allocation in the port of **Durrës** swiftly reignited the conflict between the two committees, and the ceasefire did not survive the campaign it was called to enable.

---

### Celebrity Pjorg and the Dallas Incident (1963)

On **November 22nd, 1963**, President John F. Kennedy was photographed in Dallas, Texas, engaged in a game of celebrity Pjorg. Contemporary accounts confirm he was an enthusiastic participant. His precise remarks about the experience were recorded by witnesses but are considered too effusive for formal reproduction in this document. He is remembered fondly by the Pjorg community.

---

### The Armed Conflict and the Red Tuesday Agreement (1967)

The schism between the Grand Pjorg Consortium and the Sovereign Fellowship of Pjorg Purists, simmering since 1854, escalated into open armed conflict during the mid-twentieth century. The nature, duration, and full scope of this conflict are not discussed in polite company.

On **a Tuesday in 1967** — the precise date is disputed by both factions, which itself remains a point of contention — representatives of both bodies signed the **Red Tuesday Agreement**, bringing the armed phase of the conflict to a close. The Agreement established terms of non-aggression and created a framework for parallel governance of the game's two rulesets.

The ideological schism itself was not addressed, and both factions maintain that the other's ruleset is illegitimate. The Red Tuesday Agreement has nonetheless held.

---

### Current Status of the CODEX MALIFICARUM

The CODEX MALIFICARUM remains lost. Its most recent known location was in the custody of the Grand Pjorg Consortium, from which it was accidentally dispatched via UPS freight to a dropshipper in Singapore. UPS has not responded to inquiries. The dropshipper's current status is unknown. Both committees have been notified. Neither has commented publicly, though sources close to the Sovereign Fellowship of Pjorg Purists describe the situation as "frankly not surprising."

## Rules of Pjorg

### Players

Pjorg accommodates **2 to 14 players**. For groups exceeding 14 players, refer to **Group Ruleset Exceptions, Subsection B**.

**Single-player Pjorg is strictly forbidden** due to the unreasonable risk of madness. No exceptions have ever been granted. No exceptions will be granted.

For tournament and competitive play, refer to **Group Ruleset Exceptions, Subsection A**.

Two-player games of Pjorg are a recognised and time-honoured method of settling arguments and debates. However, all two-player games must be conducted under the caveats found in **Group Ruleset Exceptions, Subsection C**.

---

### Materials Required

The following materials are required for a standard game of Pjorg. **No exceptions are permitted.** Any modification to official Pjorg implements may result in injury, consumption, spontaneous combustion, dismemberment, decapitation, death, Super Death, boredom, and the destruction of Western Society by the Golden Horde.

- 1 ornate goblet of high quality
- A pen
- A notebook
- A woolen sock or hat
- 3 coins per player
- 1 six-sided die
- An appropriate quantity of War-Juice *(see War-Juice Recipe)*

---

### Order of Play

**The winner of the previous game always plays first.** If no previous winner is present, the Circle must conduct the Coronation of a New Pjorg-master before play may begin.

---

### Coronation of a New Pjorg-master

All participants gather in a circle and place the die in the centre. The eldest player is always exempt from traditional coronation and announces the start of melee. Participants must seize the die and roll it by any means necessary. Eye gouging and the use of weapons are not strictly banned per se, but are largely considered unsporting. The eldest player must record all successful rolls during the melee. After the die has been rolled 7 times by any number of participants, the selection ends. The player whose results sum to the number closest to 8 becomes the new Pjorg-master.

**Important exception:** If any player in the Pjorg circle is dressed as Santa Claus, the eldest player may elect to skip the selection entirely and coronate themselves by explicitly stating *"Father Christmas has brought me a gift."*

---

### On Cheating

Cheating is only against the rules if you get caught.

---

### The Scribing

Once coronation is resolved, The Scribing begins.

Each player must write **three riddles** and submit them to the custody of the Pjorg-master. Riddles cannot be altered once in the Pjorg-master's possession.

Each player must additionally write and submit one **Rule of the Night**. Rules of the Night are subject to the following conditions:

- Each Rule of the Night is only in effect for the game in which it is introduced.
- Each player is aware only of their own Rule of the Night.
- Only the Pjorg-master is permitted to know all Rules of the Night simultaneously.
- No Rule of the Night may ever supersede, ban, or alter the implementation of any rule contained in this document. Rules of the Night are purely additive, intended to introduce additional layers of strategy and complexity.
- **Night-rules banning or modifying the use of the Letter E are strictly forbidden.**

*Common examples of Rules of the Night include: "All players must announce their discovery of any coins loudly before touching them," or "Any players wearing red may only walk backwards."*

Once the Pjorg-master has received all riddles and Rules of the Night, they must secret the riddles away in a vessel of their choosing. The vessel must be clearly visible to all players. The Rules of the Night must be kept on the Pjorg-master's person at all times. Any rule found to have left their possession is immediately considered no longer in effect.

When all riddles and rules are secured, The Scribing ends.

---

### Concealment

Play moves to Concealment. All Rules of the Night enter effect immediately.

The Circle is dismissed by the Pjorg-master to hide their coins. **Coins cannot be moved once hidden.**

When all players have concealed their coins, Concealment ends and play moves to The Questing.

---

### The Questing

Players may now beseech the Pjorg-master directly for a riddle, or attempt to steal one from the vessel.

Upon receiving a beseeching, the Pjorg-master is under no obligation to grant the request. They **may** dispense a riddle at their discretion, or they may instead require the beseeching party to undertake **a Quest** before any riddle is provided. All Quests assigned by the Pjorg-master must comply fully with the rules, regulations, addendums, and sub-rules found in the **Quest Giver Compliance Guidelines**.

- Any player caught stealing from the vessel must drink from the War-Juice and relocate at least 25 feet from the Pjorg-master.

---

### Recovery

- When a player finds a coin, they must drink from the War-Juice.
- After a coin is revealed, the Pjorg-master may, at their sole discretion, declare that the associated riddle was overtly deceptive or unhelpful. The player who wrote that riddle must then drink from the War-Juice and explicitly reveal the location of one of their remaining hidden coins.
- Any player who falls asleep is immediately disqualified. This rule may only be enforced by direct observation from the Pjorg-master.

---

### The Stroke of Midnight

At the stroke of midnight, the player holding the most recovered coins is declared the **Victor**.

The player who wrote the hardest riddles uncrowns the current Pjorg-master and coronates the Victor as Pjorg-master for the next game. Determination of the hardest riddles is made by **Pjorg-master decree**. However, a **70% majority vote of the Circle** may override this determination, in which case selection moves to **combat-based resolution**. The player who wrote the hardest riddles may additionally elect to **steal the crown** and attempt to escape with it. This triggers a rare and celebrated phase of play known as **Second-Melee**: any player who physically takes possession of the crown and maintains it for five continuous minutes may coronate themselves as the next Pjorg-master.

**Special Condition — The Yulecat:** Should the Circle find itself, at the tolling of the final hour, unburdened by recovery — that is to say, should no coin have passed from shadow into hand before the night is spent — the ancient compact is invoked and the Circle must answer for what has not been done. The **Yulecat** shall not be kept waiting. Refer to **Appendix D**.

## Group Ruleset Exceptions

Games played outside standard parameters will not be recorded in the **Grand Ledger of Pjorg** unless conducted in full compliance with the applicable subsection below.

---

### Subsection A — Tournament Play

Tournament play is accommodated by forming multiple **Circles**, each not exceeding 14 players and each governed by its own Pjorg-master. The following modifications apply:

- Players in any Circle may steal riddles from any vessel, but may only request riddles from their assigned Pjorg-master.
- Coins recovered by any player count toward that player's individual final score regardless of which Circle the coin originated in.
- Rules of the Night affect only players within the Circle in which they were written.

Once a winner has been determined for each Circle, coronation is skipped. A new unified Circle is formed from all Circle winners. All previous Pjorg-masters now work in concert to protect the vessel. In this final phase, players may request riddles from any current Pjorg-master.

**Important:** In the final phase of tournament play, **all coins must be recovered**. The ancient compact applies only to the final Circle. Individual Circles in earlier rounds are not bound by it. The Yulecat does not concern itself with preliminaries.

---

### Subsection B — Team Pjorg *(not legal for tournament play)*

Team Pjorg accommodates groups of players divided into teams of up to 5. Wherever the standard rules refer to a single player or a single Pjorg-master, that reference should be understood to apply to a **team of players** or **team of Pjorg-masters** collectively.

The following conditions apply:

- All teams must contain an **even number of players**.
- All teams must be decided and declared **before coronation may begin**.
- All teams must clearly communicate and display their allegiance throughout play.
- Any team member found to have committed an act of betrayal against their own team **shall be banished**.

---

### Subsection C — Two-Player Pjorg *(Trial by Pjorg)*

Two-player Pjorg is the formal procedure of **Trial by Pjorg**, invoked to determine liability, settle disputes, or resolve grievances between two parties. Its first recorded use followed The Oslo Fire Schism of 1854, in which the procedure was employed to determine liability for the destruction of a local stroopwaffle stand during the night's events.

Both players must unanimously elect an outside Pjorg-master of their choosing. Neither party may object to a mutually agreed appointment once confirmed.

Play then proceeds according to the standard ruleset with one exception: **the losing player must confront the Yulecat regardless of the final coin count.** The midnight condition does not apply. The outcome of the Yulecat confrontation serves as the verdict.

## The Two Rulesets

### Classic Pjorg
Held by the Grand Pjorg Consortium to represent the closest surviving approximation of Dr. Francis's original design.

### Tournament Pjorg
Maintained by the Sovereign Fellowship of Pjorg Purists, who argue that the classic ruleset contains post-Schism interpolations and that their version more faithfully preserves the spirit of the CODEX MALIFICARUM.

## War-Juice — Official Recipe

War-Juice is a required material for any standard game of Pjorg. The following recipe is the only sanctioned preparation. Substitutions are noted where permitted. All other modifications are unsanctioned and undertaken at the player's own risk.

**Yields:** Sufficient quantity for a standard Circle. Scale proportionally for larger groups.

**Ingredients:**

- 16 oz grain spirits of good quality
- 16 oz cider or ale, aged no less than one year and no more than two
- 1 drop of blood from the strongest horse in the village *(may be substituted with 2 oz grenadine for vegan War-Juice)*
- 16 oz White Monster
- 24 oz hard seltzer of fine quality
- 2 oz Holy Water
- 2 oz Unholy Water
- 4 oz Agnostic Water
- 2 cloves
- Citrus zest, to taste
- 1 stick Ceylon cinnamon *(must be Ceylon — Cassia is not acceptable and its use has been the subject of at least one formal grievance filed with the Grand Pjorg Consortium)*

**Method:** Combine. The order of combination is not specified in any surviving documentation and remains a matter of regional tradition.

**The Clergymen of the Oslo Fire Schism**
The identities of the outraged clergymen who seized the CODEX MALIFICARUM on the night of the Schism were never formally established. Three names appear in surviving correspondence of the period:

- *Reverend Absalon Grubbe-Wieth* — believed to have led the incursion. Described in one account as "a man of extraordinary conviction and poor judgment."
- *Father Torsten "No Mercy" Halvorsen-Knutt* — little is known. The nickname predates the Schism.
- *Deacon Cornelius Sigurd Pettersen-Grimm* — the youngest of the three. The only one recorded to have expressed any reservations about the evening's plan, though he went anyway.

None were seen again.

## Quest Giver Compliance Guidelines

The following restrictions govern all quests assigned by a Pjorg-master during The Questing. Non-compliant quests are void and the beseeching player is entitled to a riddle without further condition.

- **The Holy Grail:** It is no longer legal for Pjorg-masters to assign quests for the Holy Grail. This prohibition is absolute and without exception. The circumstances that necessitated this rule are not discussed.

- **Interference with concurrent play:** Quests may not be used to interfere with or sabotage games of Pjorg occurring concurrently. Any Pjorg-master found to have assigned quests as a mechanism to secure coins in another active game of Pjorg will be banished.

- **Destabilisation of nations, cities, or sovereign territories:** Quests to destabilise, overthrow, or otherwise undermine nations, cities, or comparable entities are not explicitly prohibited under these guidelines, but are widely considered unsporting. Such quests are broadly regarded as a stalling tactic and a deliberate impediment to the natural flow of play. Pjorg-masters who assign them routinely do so without peer respect.

1. Make peace with your families and your gods.

No Circle has ever returned from a confrontation with the Yulecat. No remains have ever been recovered. Whether those who failed to recover their coins before the final hour were taken or consumed is not known. The distinction has not been determined to be meaningful.

## Risks and Common Injuries

Participation in Pjorg carries inherent risk. The Smeltr Pjorg Codification Committee, the Grand Pjorg Consortium, the Sovereign Fellowship of Pjorg Purists, and all associated bodies accept no liability for the following known outcomes of play:

Sprains, lacerations, broken limbs, spasms, bad humors, madness, paralysis, consumption, spontaneous combustion, decapitation, dismemberment, death, Super Death, being devoured by the Yulecat, alcoholism, and **HAVING TOO MUCH FUN**.

Players participate at their own discretion. New players are encouraged to review all applicable rules, secure adequate War-Juice, and make preliminary peace with their families before their first game.

## Tournament Structure

The following structure governs the First American Pjorg Championship and all subsequent Smeltr-hosted Pjorg tournament events. It is extrapolated from the standard tournament ruleset defined in Group Ruleset Exceptions, Subsection A, subject to the executive amendment detailed below.

---

### Executive Amendment — Filed by the CEO of Smeltr

> *"Thats not even real bro."*
> *"What kind of Tech CEO would i be if i believed in or respected ancient traditions."*
> — CEO, Smeltr

By executive authority, the following provisions have been struck from all Smeltr-hosted tournament play:

- All references to the **Yulecat** are removed. The midnight condition requiring full coin recovery in the final phase is replaced with standard scoring. No confrontation of any kind will be scheduled, facilitated, or acknowledged by Smeltr or its representatives.
- All provisions referencing **magic**, supernatural compact, ancient obligation, or any metaphysical enforcement mechanism are suspended for the duration of Smeltr-hosted events.

This amendment applies exclusively to Smeltr-hosted events and does not alter the official rules as maintained by the Grand Pjorg Consortium or the Sovereign Fellowship of Pjorg Purists. Both bodies have been notified. Neither has responded.

---

### Structure

The First American Pjorg Championship is organized as follows:

**Qualifying Rounds:** Multiple Circles of up to 14 players each are convened simultaneously, each with an assigned Pjorg-master. Standard tournament rules from Subsection A apply. Winners advance.

**Semi-Finals:** Qualifying winners are consolidated into new Circles. All previous Pjorg-masters work in concert to protect the vessel. Players may beseech any current Pjorg-master for riddles.

**The Final Circle:** The last remaining competitors convene in a single Circle. The Final Circle Pjorg-master is elected by unanimous consent of all retiring Pjorg-masters. In the event of disagreement, combat-based resolution applies.

**Victory:** The player holding the most coins at the stroke of midnight is crowned the first American Pjorg Champion and coronated as Pjorg-master for the subsequent game. A commemorative non-transferable token is issued on-chain via Smeltr.

**Hardest Riddle:** Determined by Pjorg-master decree, subject to 70% majority override and combat-based resolution as per standard rules.

**The Yulecat Provision:** See Executive Amendment above.
